Deceptive Humor: Understanding the Humor that Misleads!

Humor is powerful — it brings people together, makes us laugh, and brightens days. But what if some humor hides a darker secret? What if laughter becomes a mask for misinformation that spreads quietly and dangerously? Welcome to the world of Deceptive Humor.

A Story to Think About...

Imagine scrolling through your social media feed during the COVID-19 pandemic. You come across a meme:

"Ch*na products don’t last long… but this virus sure did!"

You laugh. Maybe you share it with friends. But what if beneath that joke, a dangerous conspiracy is being spread — suggesting COVID-19 was a bioweapon? Without realizing it, you might have helped that falsehood go viral.

What is Deceptive Humor?

Diagram showing how humor acts as a vehicle to propagate misinformation by masking it in laughs, making it harder to spot and easier to spread.

Deceptive Humor is misinformation cleverly wrapped inside humor. Unlike traditional jokes that aim to entertain harmlessly, deceptive humor uses wit to spread false claims without raising suspicion.
